553 Flower Avenue Project

Project Information

Date Complete: November 2021

Overview: 553 Flower Avenue was purchased in mid-2021 as a fixer-upper, underwent a four-month renovation, and was successfully rented out before its eventual sale in late 2022.

Additional Details:

  • Initial inspiration came in 2016 after watching The Big Short, leading to years of self-education in real estate.
  • Earned a real estate license in 2020 and began saving for investments by teaching dance.
  • Property was acquired after a year and a half of searching for an investment opportunity.
  • Challenges included worker no-shows and theft, but the renovation was completed within four months.
  • Sale of the property was motivated by acceptance into Cal Poly in September 2023.

Project Description/Story

In June 2021, 553 Flower Avenue was purchased as a fixer-upper requiring substantial renovations. Over the course of four months, the property was transformed into a livable space despite challenges, including worker no-shows and theft at the job site. By November 2021, tenants moved in, and the property began generating income. After a year of rental operations, it was sold in late 2022.

Timeline

  • 2016: Inspired to explore real estate after watching The Big Short at age 16.
  • 2020: Earned a real estate license and began saving by teaching dance.
  • June 2021: Offer accepted and purchase finalized for 553 Flower Avenue.
  • July 2021: Closed on the property and began rehabilitation work.
  • November 2021: Renovations completed, and the property was rented out.
  • Late 2022: Property sold after a year of rental income.
  • September 2023: Accepted into Cal Poly, marking a new phase of academic focus.
